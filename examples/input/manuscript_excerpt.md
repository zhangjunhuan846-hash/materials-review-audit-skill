# Biomass-derived carbon materials for electrochemical energy storage

The final sample-level records were LIB 101, SIB 92, and aqueous SC 154. These databases support a cross-system comparison of biomass-derived carbon materials.

In Fig.7, BET surface area is positively correlated with SIB ICE and therefore drives higher initial Coulombic efficiency in sodium-ion batteries.

The d002 spacing shows a significantly positive correlation with SIB ICE, indicating that enlarged interlayer spacing improves the initial efficiency.

High BET generally improves aqueous supercapacitor capacitance, but in organic-electrolyte batteries excessive open surface area may reduce ICE through increased SEI formation.

The descriptor database contains BET, d002, ID/IG, XPS N/O, total pore volume, mass loading, electrode thickness, compacted density, ICE, capacity, and capacitance values.
